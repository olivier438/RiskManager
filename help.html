Getting started
What is Risk Manager?

Risk Manager is a sovereign, AI-powered GRC platform designed for Belgian public institutions and NIS2-regulated entities. It allows you to identify, analyse, treat and monitor information security risks — all within a single, secure environment.

Risk Manager is built by ONE CIRCLE IT SOLUTIONS and hosted on Infomaniak infrastructure in Switzerland — outside the scope of the US CLOUD Act.

How do I log in?

Go to your organisation's Risk Manager URL and enter your email and password on the login page. Your account is created by your Risk Leader or Administrator.

If you have not received credentials, contact your internal Risk Manager administrator.

Security: Sessions expire automatically after 5 minutes of inactivity. You will be redirected to the login page.
What are the different user roles?

Risk Manager has three roles:

  • Risk Leader — Full access. Validates analyses, approves treatment decisions, manages users and environment settings. Typically the CISO or Risk Manager.
  • Analyst — Works on assigned risks. Fills in likelihood, impact, scenario, controls. Submits for review. Cannot create risks.
  • Risk Owner — Creates risks and submits them to the analysis pool. Cannot modify risk scores or approve treatments.
Note: Role permissions are enforced server-side via Row Level Security — they cannot be bypassed from the browser.
What is an Environment?

An Environment is your organisation's isolated workspace in Risk Manager. All risks, users, and settings are scoped to your environment — no data can cross between environments.

Each environment has its own: name, NIS2 sector, risk appetite (1–5), active frameworks (NIS2/CyFun, ISO 27001, DORA) and Risk Studio data sources.

Risk management
How do I create a new risk?

Click the ⊕ button in the left sidebar or the + New Risk button in the My Risks panel. Fill in at minimum:

  • Risk Name — clear, descriptive title
  • Description — plain language description (tags are auto-detected)
  • Likelihood and Impact (1–5 scale)

As you type the description, Risk Manager automatically suggests framework measures (NIS2 or ISO 27001) with technical and GRC advice matched to your risk context.

Tip: Save as Draft to refine later, or Submit for Analysis to send directly to the analyst pool.
What is the risk workflow (statuses)?

Every risk follows a structured lifecycle:

  • DRAFT — Created, private by default. Only the creator can see it.
  • IN ANALYSIS — Submitted to the pool. An analyst takes ownership.
  • IN REVIEW — Analysis complete, submitted for Risk Leader approval. Visibility switches to TEAM.
  • IN TREATMENT — Approved. Treatment decision (Reduce / Accept / Transfer) in progress.
  • MONITORED — Treatment applied or accepted. Risk is tracked over time.
  • TREATED — Treatment confirmed and closed.

Each transition is logged with the author and timestamp — full audit trail.

How is the Gross Risk score calculated?

The Gross Risk score is calculated automatically: Likelihood × Impact.

Severity levels:

  • Critical — score ≥ 16
  • High — score 9–15
  • Medium — score 4–8
  • Low — score 1–3

The 5×5 risk matrix in the Analysis tab visualises your position visually with a dot marker.

What are the treatment decisions?
  • Reduce — Implement controls to lower likelihood or impact.
  • Accept — Formally accept the residual risk (documented, traceable).
  • Transfer — Transfer the risk to a third party (insurance, supplier contract).

The decision is set during the IN TREATMENT phase and must be confirmed by a Risk Leader before the risk moves to MONITORED.

What is the Analysis Journal?

The Analysis Journal is a timestamped log of notes added by analysts and risk leaders during the risk lifecycle. Accessible via the Journal tab in the risk detail modal.

Each entry records: author, date/time, and free-text note. Journal entries are immutable — they cannot be deleted, ensuring full auditability.

Best practice: Log all significant decisions and observations in the journal. This is your audit evidence.
What is PRIVATE vs TEAM visibility?
  • PRIVATE — Only the creator can see the risk. Default at creation.
  • TEAM — All members of your environment can see the risk. Automatically switches when a risk moves to IN REVIEW.

There is no PUBLIC visibility in Risk Manager — data never leaves your environment.

Framework measures & AI
How does the automatic measure suggestion work?

As you type the risk description, Risk Manager analyses the text and detects keywords (in French and English). It then matches them against a curated database of NIS2 and ISO 27001 measures.

Each suggested measure comes with two levels of advice:

  • TECH — Concrete technical implementation recommendations
  • GRC — Policy, governance and documentation recommendations

You can check or uncheck each measure. Only the checked ones are saved with the risk.

Sovereign AI: This analysis runs on Infomaniak infrastructure in Switzerland. No data leaves the EU/CH.
Which frameworks are supported?

Risk Manager currently supports:

  • NIS2 Directive — 19 security domains (access control, vulnerability management, continuity, incident response, etc.)
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Annex A controls (A.5.x through A.8.x)
  • DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Act (coming)

Active frameworks are configured per environment by your Risk Leader.

Where can I see the measures saved on a risk?

Open any risk by clicking on it in the dashboard. In the risk detail modal, go to the Overview tab. The Framework Measures section displays all measures that were saved, grouped by framework (NIS2 / ISO 27001), with their TECH and GRC advice.

Threat Marketplace & Risk Studio
What is Risk Studio?

Risk Studio is the threat intelligence platform by ONE CIRCLE IT SOLUTIONS. It continuously collects and enriches cybersecurity threats, CVEs, data leaks and GRC-related risks from public sources.

Risk Manager connects to Risk Studio in read-only mode — your internal risks never flow back to Risk Studio.

How do I use the Threat Marketplace?

Click the Threat Marketplace icon (grid of squares) in the left sidebar, or navigate to the Search page and click the Threat Marketplace tab.

Browse threats by category:

  • CYBERSEC — CVEs, malware, attack techniques
  • DATALEAK — data breaches and exfiltration events
  • GRC — regulatory and compliance risks

Click ⊕ Add → on any threat to import it as a DRAFT risk in your register.

What is the Live Feed on the dashboard?

The Risk Studio Live Feed on the right of the dashboard shows the 10 most recent threats from Risk Studio. The Global Threat Pulse indicator at the top signals the current threat level:

  • CALM — No significant global activity
  • ELEVATED — Abnormal activity detected
  • CRITICAL — Major global incident in progress

Click any feed item to see details and import it as a risk.

Navigation & KPI
What does the sidebar do?

The vertical sidebar on the left is your main navigation and quick-action bar:

  • Radar logo — Return to Dashboard
  • ⊕ — Create a new risk
  • ⌕ — Search the risk register
  • Bar chart — KPI Dashboard
  • Grid — Threat Marketplace
  • Triangle — Declare an Incident (coming in V2)
  • P1 / P2 / P3 / P4 / D — Filter risks by severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low / Draft)
  • ? — This help page
  • ⚙ — Settings (Risk Leader only)
What KPIs are available on the KPI Dashboard?

The KPI Dashboard provides real-time metrics from your risk register:

  • Total Active / Critical / High — Risk counts by severity
  • Treatment Rate — % of risks in MONITORED or TREATED status
  • Avg Gross Score — Mean Likelihood × Impact across all risks
  • Pending Action — Risks in IN ANALYSIS or IN REVIEW
  • Risks Closed — MONITORED + TREATED
  • Overdue — Active risks with no activity for more than 30 days
  • Avg. Resolution — Average days from creation to MONITORED status
  • Imported from RS — Risks pulled from Risk Studio

Charts show risk evolution over 12 months, distribution by status and severity, top 5 highest-scored risks, risks by owner, and the treatment pipeline.

How do I switch the interface language?

Click the language button (EN / FR / NL / DE) in the top-right corner of the topbar. The interface switches immediately. Your preference is saved locally and remembered on next login.

Note: Risk data (names, descriptions) you've entered stays in the language you typed it in — only the interface labels are translated.
Security & compliance
Where is my data stored?

All data is hosted on Infomaniak infrastructure, based in Switzerland and France. Infomaniak is ISO 27001 certified, GDPR-compliant, and outside the scope of the US CLOUD Act.

Risk Manager uses a dedicated PostgreSQL database per environment with Row Level Security (RLS) enforced — no cross-environment data access is possible.

Is my session secure?

Yes. Risk Manager uses JWT-based authentication via Supabase Auth with the following protections:

  • Auto-refresh tokens — Sessions stay valid without requiring re-login during active use
  • Inactivity timeout — After 5 minutes without activity, you are automatically logged out
  • HTTPS only — All traffic is encrypted in transit
  • Server-side validation — Every authenticated request is validated server-side via getUser()
Is Risk Manager NIS2 compliant?

Risk Manager is designed to support NIS2 compliance — it is not itself subject to NIS2 as a tool, but it helps your organisation meet NIS2 Article 21 requirements by:

  • Maintaining a structured risk register with full audit trail
  • Mapping risks to NIS2 security domains (access control, continuity, incident management, etc.)
  • Tracking treatment decisions with accountability
  • Providing measurable KPIs for governance reporting
Coming in V2: Full incident management with CCB notification workflow (H+24 / H+72 / J+30) to complete the NIS2 lifecycle end-to-end.
